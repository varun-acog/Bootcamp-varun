Exercise 6: Redesign of the Websites

Level 1: Identifying Problems and Suggestions

1. Indian Government Press Releases

Problems:

  • Lacks a clean, user-friendly layout. The text is cramped, making it difficult to read.
  • No visual hierarchy to emphasize important announcements.
  • Poor navigation system; finding press releases by date or topic is cumbersome.

Inspiration: The US Department of State website.

  • Clean, professional design with clear headings and ample white space.
  • Interactive filters and categories for easier navigation.

2. IITM CSE Faculty Site

Problems:

  • Outdated design with poor font choices and lack of visual appeal.
  • Faculty details are not consistently formatted.

Inspiration: MIT EECS Faculty Page.

  • Well-organized layout with uniform formatting for faculty profiles.
  • Clickable profile cards with detailed information in a modal or new page.

3. IIT B Talk

Problems:

  • Overloaded with text and no emphasis on visuals or hierarchy.
  • Lack of an intuitive menu or search feature for talks.

Inspiration: TED Talks Website.

  • Visually engaging design with thumbnails and concise descriptions.
  • Filters and recommendations for seamless navigation.

4. Indian Railways Website

Problems:

  • Cluttered design with too many elements on the home page.
  • Lacks modern features like predictive search and a responsive layout.

Inspiration: Deutsche Bahn (German Railways).

  • Simplified design with a focus on search and journey planning.
  • Responsive layout with smooth performance on mobile devices.

Level 2: Comparative Analysis

1. Flipkart vs Amazon (Mobile Phones Page)

Design Comparison:

  • Flipkart: Brighter colors, slightly overwhelming with ads and promotions. Navigation is straightforward but not as polished.
  • Amazon: Cleaner layout with better use of white space. Product categories and filters are more user-friendly.

Service Perspective:

  • Amazon offers a wider range of products with extensive reviews and recommendations.
  • Flipkart excels in region-specific deals and promotions.

2. Telangana State vs Michigan State Websites

Design Comparison:

  • Telangana: Basic design with limited interactive elements. Navigation is functional but lacks visual appeal.
  • Michigan: Professional design with intuitive navigation and rich media content.

Services Perspective:

  • Michigan's website emphasizes public engagement and accessibility.
  • Telangana's website focuses more on static information with minimal interaction.

Level 3: Redesign

1. Redesign of Indian Government Press Releases

Key Changes:

  • Simplified layout with a focus on important press releases.
  • Filters for date and category with a search bar at the top.
  • Improved typography and spacing for readability.

2. Redesign of IITM CSE Faculty Site

Key Changes:

  • Uniform profile cards with photos and brief descriptions.
  • Search and filter options for faculty by area of expertise.
  • Modern design with responsive features for mobile devices.

Key Learnings and Challenges

Learnings:

  • Effective web design emphasizes clarity, usability, and accessibility.
  • Inspiration from well-designed websites helps improve aesthetics and functionality.

Challenges:

  • Balancing aesthetic appeal with functionality.
  • Ensuring compatibility across different devices and browsers.
  • Choosing the right visual hierarchy to enhance user experience.
